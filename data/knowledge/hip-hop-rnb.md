# Hip-Hop and R&B Guide

## Classic Hip-Hop
Classic hip-hop emerged from New York City in the late 1970s, built on four foundational elements: DJing, MCing (rapping), breakdancing, and graffiti art. Early pioneers include DJ Kool Herc, Grandmaster Flash, and Afrika Bambaataa. The golden age (late 1980s to mid-1990s) produced defining works from Public Enemy, N.W.A, A Tribe Called Quest, and Nas, characterised by complex sampling, socially conscious lyrics, and strong regional identities.

## Boom Bap
Boom bap is a style of hip-hop production defined by a hard-hitting kick-snare pattern, heavy use of jazz and soul samples, and a raw, unpolished aesthetic. It emerged in New York in the late 1980s and early 1990s and is closely associated with producers like DJ Premier, Pete Rock, and Large Professor. Artists include Gang Starr, Biggie, and Rakim.

## Cloud Rap and Lo-Fi Hip-Hop
Cloud rap emerged in the early 2010s as a hazy, introspective subgenre influenced by chopped-and-screwed techniques and ambient music. It features slow tempos, heavy reverb, pitch-shifted vocals, and a dreamlike quality. Lil B, Main Attrakionz, and A$AP Mob were early figures. Lo-fi hip-hop (popularised on YouTube study streams) is a mellower derivative featuring dusty vinyl samples, jazz chords, and slow, relaxed beats.

## Contemporary R&B
Contemporary R&B blends rhythm and blues with soul, hip-hop, and electronic production. It emerged in the 1990s through artists like TLC, Boyz II Men, and Usher, and has evolved through decades of influence. Frank Ocean's channel ORANGE and Blonde expanded the genre's emotional and sonic range. Current figures include SZA, Daniel Caesar, Steve Lacy, and Brent Faiyaz. The genre is characterised by lush production, confessional lyrics, and vocal technique.

## Alternative R&B
Alternative R&B, sometimes called PBR&B, blends R&B with indie, electronic, and experimental elements. It emerged in the early 2010s through artists like Frank Ocean, The Weeknd, and Solange. It tends to be more introspective and sonically adventurous than mainstream R&B, often incorporating unconventional structures, jazz harmony, and lo-fi aesthetics.

## Trap
Trap music originated in Atlanta in the early 2000s and has become the dominant force in mainstream hip-hop. It is defined by 808 drum machines, hi-hat rolls, heavy bass, and lyrics about street life and ambition. T.I., Gucci Mane, and Young Jeezy established the genre. Producers like Lex Luger, Metro Boomin, and Zaytoven shaped its sound. Post-Malone and Drake brought trap production into pop crossover territory.
