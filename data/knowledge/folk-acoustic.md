# Folk and Acoustic Music Guide

## Singer-Songwriter
The singer-songwriter tradition centres on solo artists who write and perform their own material, typically with acoustic guitar or piano. It flourished in the early 1970s through figures like Joni Mitchell, James Taylor, and Cat Stevens. The genre values personal, confessional lyrics and intimate performance over technical complexity. Elliott Smith, Nick Drake, and Iron & Wine represent its darker, more introspective branch.

## Freak Folk
Freak folk (or psychedelic folk) emerged in the early 2000s as a loose movement of artists blending traditional folk instrumentation with experimental, avant-garde sensibilities. It draws on the 1960s acid-folk tradition (Nick Drake, Vashti Bunyan, Incredible String Band) and adds contemporary sonic exploration. Key artists include Devendra Banhart, Joanna Newsom, Sufjan Stevens, and Antony and the Johnsons.

## Americana and Alt-Country
Americana is a broad category encompassing American roots music — country, folk, bluegrass, blues, and rock — performed with a roots-conscious sensibility. It developed in the 1990s as an alternative to mainstream Nashville country. Artists like Wilco, Ryan Adams, Gillian Welch, and Lucinda Williams blended country forms with rock production and literary songwriting. The sound values authenticity, narrative, and regional identity.

## British Folk Revival
The British folk revival of the 1960s and 1970s saw artists such as Fairport Convention, Pentangle, and Bert Jansch revitalising traditional English, Scottish, and Irish folk traditions with electric instruments and rock arrangements. The movement produced a distinctive sound: acoustic guitar fingerpicking, modal harmonies, and songs drawn from folk tradition. Nick Drake's pastoral chamber folk emerged from this milieu.

## Indie Folk
Indie folk emerged in the 2000s as a softer, more melodic branch of indie rock. It features acoustic instruments, gentle tempos, and intimate production, often incorporating orchestral arrangements. Artists include Sufjan Stevens, Iron & Wine, Fleet Foxes, Bon Iver, and The Decemberists. Bon Iver's For Emma, Forever Ago (2008) — recorded in a Wisconsin cabin — is a touchstone of the genre's lo-fi, confessional aesthetic.
