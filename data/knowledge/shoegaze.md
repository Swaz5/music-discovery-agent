## Shoegaze

### What It Sounds Like
Shoegaze is the sound of guitars dissolving into each other. Take four or five guitar tracks, drench every one of them in reverb, chorus, and tremolo, stack them until no single note is identifiable, and add vocals so hushed and buried in the mix that they become just another texture. The result is a "wall of sound" that feels less like a band playing songs and more like a weather system moving through your chest.

Tempos are typically mid-range — not slow, not fast, but propulsive in a way that feels inevitable rather than urgent. Drums hit hard but are swallowed by the guitars. Basslines often carry more melodic weight than the lead guitar. The dynamic range is extreme: a track will cruise at a quiet hum before the guitars detonate into something overwhelming, then recede again just as suddenly. Everything is turned inward.

The name came from the British music press mocking how bands spent shows staring at their pedalboards instead of engaging the audience. They weren't wrong. This is introverted music, built for headphones.

### Key Artists
- **My Bloody Valentine** — the non-negotiable centre of gravity. *Loveless* (1991) remains the definitive statement: Kevin Shields spent millions of dollars getting guitars to sound like that.
- **Slowdive** — softer and more melodic than MBV, with Rachel Goswell and Neil Halstead's harmonies riding above the haze. *Souvlaki* is heartbreaking in the best way.
- **Ride** — the most rock-oriented of the core bands; *Nowhere* has a propulsive energy that makes it accessible without sacrificing the density.
- **Lush** — brought strong pop songwriting instincts to the genre; *Spooky* is the most accessible entry point.
- **Chapterhouse** — underrated and warmly melodic; *Whirlpool* deserves more attention than it gets.
- **Moose** — even more overlooked, but worth finding for anyone who goes deep.
- **Deafheaven** — brought shoegaze into black metal territory on *Sunbather*; proved the wall-of-sound approach works at extreme volumes.

### Subgenres & Related Styles
Dream pop is the closest relative — same reverb-heavy aesthetic but with the distortion dialled back and melody pushed forward. **Beach House** and **Cocteau Twins** live here. Slowcore (Low, Red House Painters) borrows the inward quality and the dynamics but strips away the guitar density. Blackgaze (Deafheaven, Alcest) fuses shoegaze texture with black metal aggression. Post-rock (Mogwai, Godspeed You! Black Emperor) shares the loud-quiet-loud architecture. If you're arriving from indie rock, try Ride first; if you're coming from electronic music, Slowdive will make most sense.

### The Vibe
Late at night, alone, with good headphones. Long train journeys where the window becomes a screen. That specific kind of melancholy that isn't depression — more like a beautiful ache. Shoegaze is for when you want to be overwhelmed in a way that feels safe. It pairs with: reading literary fiction, grey autumn weather, the first hour after a relationship ends when everything still feels cinematic.
