# Alternative and Indie Rock Guide

## Shoegaze
Shoegaze emerged in the UK in the late 1980s. The name comes from performers staring at their effects pedals during performances. It is defined by a thick "wall of sound" created by multiple overlapping guitar tracks drenched in reverb and delay, hushed vocals treated as textural instruments, and loud-quiet dynamics. My Bloody Valentine's Loveless (1991) is the genre's landmark album. Other key artists: Slowdive, Ride, Lush, Chapterhouse.

## Dream Pop
Dream pop is a subgenre of alternative rock and post-punk that emphasises mood and atmosphere over traditional song structure. It uses lush production, reverb-heavy guitars, ethereal vocals, and slow tempos to create an introspective, melancholic quality. It overlaps significantly with shoegaze and 4AD's aesthetic. Key artists include Cocteau Twins, Beach House, Mazzy Star, and Chromatics.

## Post-Punk
Post-punk emerged in the late 1970s as a reaction against punk's perceived limitations. It incorporated elements of funk, dub, art rock, and electronic music into punk's raw energy. The genre is angular, intellectual, and experimental — characterised by bass-driven grooves, choppy guitar work, and often bleak or oblique lyrics. Bands include Joy Division, Wire, Gang of Four, Television, and The Fall.

## Indie Rock
Indie rock developed in the 1980s as guitar-based music produced and distributed independently of major record labels. It spans a wide sonic range but is unified by a DIY ethos and non-commercial intent. By the 1990s it encompassed lo-fi (Pavement, Guided by Voices), slacker rock, and the Britpop movement (Oasis, Blur, Pulp). Contemporary indie rock includes The National, Vampire Weekend, and Arcade Fire.

## Grunge
Grunge emerged from Seattle in the late 1980s, blending heavy metal guitar tones with punk rock energy and alternative sensibilities. It is characterised by distorted guitars, angst-filled lyrics, and a deliberately unkempt aesthetic. Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains defined the genre. Grunge brought alternative rock to mainstream audiences in the early 1990s.

## Psychedelic Rock
Psychedelic rock emerged in the mid-1960s as musicians began incorporating studio experimentation and mind-expanding drug culture into rock music. Features include unconventional song structures, unusual instruments (sitar, mellotron), tape effects, and surreal lyrics. The Beatles, The Doors, Jefferson Airplane, and Pink Floyd pioneered the genre. Modern psychedelia includes Tame Impala, MGMT, and King Gizzard and the Lizard Wizard.
