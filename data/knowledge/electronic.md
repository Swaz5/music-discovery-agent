# Electronic Music Guide

## House Music
House music originated in Chicago in the early 1980s, emerging from the disco era. It is characterised by a steady four-on-the-floor kick drum pattern, off-beat hi-hats, synthesised basslines, and soulful or sampled vocals. Sub-genres include deep house, tech house, and Chicago house. Key artists include Frankie Knuckles, Larry Heard, and Derrick May.

## Techno
Detroit techno developed in the mid-1980s as a blend of electronic music influences including Kraftwerk, electro, and funk. It features mechanical, industrial rhythms, synthesised sounds, and a futuristic aesthetic. Unlike house, techno is generally more minimal and abstract, designed for continuous DJ mixing. Pioneers include Juan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson.

## Ambient Electronic
Ambient electronic music prioritises texture and atmosphere over conventional song structure. Pioneered by Brian Eno in the late 1970s, it uses synthesisers, tape loops, and studio effects to create immersive soundscapes. The music is intended as environmental — it rewards both active listening and passive background use. Artists include Aphex Twin, The Orb, and Boards of Canada.

## Trip-Hop
Trip-hop emerged from Bristol, UK in the early 1990s, blending hip-hop beats with elements of jazz, soul, and electronic production. It is characterised by slow, heavy breakbeats, melancholic atmospheres, and introspective lyrics. The genre is defined by artists such as Massive Attack, Portishead, and Tricky. The sound is dark, cinematic, and deeply moody.

## IDM (Intelligent Dance Music)
IDM is an umbrella term for electronic music that prioritises complex, experimental sound design over dancefloor functionality. It features intricate rhythms, unconventional structures, and detailed sonic textures. Warp Records was central to the genre's development. Key artists include Aphex Twin, Autechre, Squarepusher, and Plaid.

## French House
French house is a style of house music that emerged in Paris in the mid-1990s. It is distinguished by its use of heavy filtering effects on samples — particularly the bandpass filter sweep that gives tracks a distinctive "wah" quality — and funky, groove-driven loops. Daft Punk, Cassius, and Stardust are central figures.
