## Dream Pop

### What It Sounds Like
Dream pop is the gentle version of shoegaze — same reverb-soaked atmosphere, same preference for mood over melody, but the distortion has been replaced with something softer and more luminous. Where shoegaze is a wall, dream pop is a mist. Guitars shimmer rather than roar. Vocals float above the arrangement rather than being buried in it. The tempos are slow to mid-range and the rhythms feel cushioned, unhurried.

Production tends toward the lush: synth pads underneath guitars, reverb on everything, a spaciousness that makes even small ensembles sound large. There's a recurring quality of suspension — dream pop songs often feel like they're hovering, resisting resolution, happy to stay inside a mood rather than moving toward a climax. This is music that earns its name: it genuinely sounds like a dream, with the logic of one too (emotional coherence without strict narrative structure).

The 4AD Records aesthetic defined the genre's early shape — a label whose house style was so distinctive that "4AD band" became its own meaningful descriptor. Cold, beautiful, slightly inhuman, exquisitely produced.

### Key Artists
- **Cocteau Twins** — the originators and the ceiling. Elizabeth Fraser's voice is so processed it becomes its own instrument; the music is untranslatable to any other context.
- **Beach House** — the contemporary heart of the genre. Victoria Legrand's organ and Alex Scally's guitar have defined dream pop for a generation; *Teen Dream* and *Depression Cherry* are essential.
- **Mazzy Star** — Hope Sandoval's drowsy vocals over David Roback's country-inflected guitar; *So Tonight That I Might See* is devastating in slow motion.
- **Grouper** — the loneliest version of the genre; Liz Harris records with a cassette tape warmth that makes everything sound like it's being transmitted from somewhere very far away.
- **Washed Out** — the chillwave intersection, bright and nostalgic rather than melancholic.
- **Chromatics** — darker, synthier, more nocturnal; the genre's Hollywood noir wing.
- **The xx** — stripped to almost nothing: two guitars, a bass, and space between notes. *xx* is one of the most intimate albums of the 2010s.

### Subgenres & Related Styles
Dream pop is in constant dialogue with **shoegaze** (add distortion), **ambient** (remove the songs), and **slowcore** (slow down and drain the colour). **Chillwave** (Washed Out, Toro y Moi) is a sunnier, more nostalgic offshoot. **Ethereal wave** (Dead Can Dance, This Mortal Coil) is dream pop's goth-adjacent sibling. Shoegaze fans almost always love dream pop; the reverse is also true. If you know Beach House and want to go deeper, start with Cocteau Twins' *Heaven or Las Vegas* and work backwards.

### The Vibe
Dream pop is for the golden hour. It's Sunday morning music, late afternoon in autumn, the mood of somewhere between sleep and waking. It's unusually good for emotional regulation — the sustained atmosphere gives feelings room to exist without being pressed toward resolution. Activities: slow cooking, reading poetry, long baths, the commute home after a day that was quietly good.
